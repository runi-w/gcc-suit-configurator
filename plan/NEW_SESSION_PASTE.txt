SETTLED — do not relitigate:
- 2D compositing, NOT real-time 3D. Proven from live production assets that Suitsupply and
  Hockerty are both pre-rendered 2D layer stacks. Don't propose 3D.
- Do NOT add AO / crevice darkening in folds. Tried, rejected, and Suitsupply measurably have none.
- Our asset count is fabric-independent (we composite cloth at runtime). ~126 renders covers every
  visible option across all 117 cloths. Do NOT copy Suitsupply's 25,000-layer per-fabric approach.
- Fabric names come from Shopify, never guessed from colour.
- Suits are made-to-measure — never "handcrafted" or "hand-tailored".

MEASUREMENT RULES (each of these already cost a wasted round):
- Hard-mask alpha>200, and erode 3px on a variance mask. The anti-aliased edge band inflated
  measured specular from 3.3 to 10.9 and looked like a real defect.
- Compare images at MATCHED px/cm. Mismatched density made our pattern look 2x wobblier than it is.
- Use Suitsupply's `ai-generated/ai-model` layer as reference, never their flat `Jacket/model`
  layers — a garment with no body inside has neckline-cavity darks ours can't have.
- audit/period.py is unreliable (locks onto harmonics). Settle scale questions visually.
- Metrics miss things. An edge-roughness metric rated a broken mask identical to a good one; only
  a 6x visual zoom found it. Always look at the image, don't just trust the number.
