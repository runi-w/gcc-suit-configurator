  THEN port the prototype into the compositor (buildWarpNormal / warpedCloth), reusing the
  disabled SIL_WRAP machinery. Cover all directional patterns (stripe/check/windowpane/glen).
Tasks #9 (R1/R3) and #10 (R2) are already created for this.

THE CRUX QUESTION the research must answer (per panel): is the apparent stripe tilt driven by
(a) the 3D surface (normal-driven), (b) the panel's silhouette-width taper (the SIL_WRAP model),
or (c) a deliberate cut grain? Likely a mix. Getting this wrong sends the implementation the wrong
way. Torso is already at SS parity — do NOT regress it.

SETTLED — do not relitigate:
- 2D compositing, NOT real-time 3D (proven: SS + Hockerty are baked 2D layer stacks).
- Do NOT add AO / crevice darkening in folds (tried, rejected; SS folds are flatter than ours).
- Torso pattern wrap is ALREADY at SS parity. Global `SIL_WRAP` is rejected (shears sleeves with
  no per-panel segmentation) — Path A fixes that by making it per-panel.
- Crease depth's lever is the COMPOSITOR (soften_drape), not the base render. Fixed.
- SS's "clean pins" are drawn ~2× physical line-width — a display idealisation, not wrap.
- Fabric names come from Shopify, never guessed from colour.
- AI cannot do the garment CAD (researched July 2026). Suits are made-to-measure — never
  "handcrafted" / "hand-tailored".

MEASUREMENT RULES (each already cost a wasted round):
- Compare pattern at MATCHED px/cm (raw 49° vs 23° was a resolution artifact; at matched density
  it's parity). Resample to a common density first.
- Use Suitsupply's `ai-generated/ai-model` layer as reference, never their flat garment layers.
- audit/period.py is unreliable — settle scale/wrap questions VISUALLY.
- Metrics miss things; always LOOK at the image (6× zoom), don't just trust the number.
- Hard-mask alpha>200, erode ~3px on a variance mask when measuring composites.
