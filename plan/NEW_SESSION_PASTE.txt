Two mechanisms cover the whole garment, not three. SIL_WRAP (silhouette-width taper) is CONFIRMED
DEAD everywhere measured — do not resurrect it, even per-panel.

  Panel        Mechanism                              Target               Confidence
  -----------  -------------------------------------  -------------------  ----------------------
  Torso L/R    existing normal-driven warp, UNCHANGED at SS parity          solved (don't regress)
  Lapel L/R    NEW fixed-angle rotation, mirrored     ±11° FLOOR (→15-30°)  medium (tune by eye)
  Sleeve L/R   VERIFY existing mechanism first        ±5° FLOOR             medium
  Collar       NEW fixed-angle rotation               ~85-90° (near-horiz)  medium (bias-cut)
  Back         assume = torso (NO data/asset)         —                     none
  Pockets      inherit the panel they sit on          n/a                   low stakes

SEAM RULES (R1, best-grounded part):
  - Center-back: collar-continuity PHASE MATCH, not a mirror (two back halves sit one repeat apart
    at the collar, gap varies down the back).
  - Sleeve-to-body: match ONE prominent line at the chest/armhole junction only; rest is free.
  - Front opening: leave the existing SEAM_PHASE break as-is (unresolved in R1, don't spend budget).
  - Pockets: genuinely disputed between bespoke houses — inherit panel grain, don't force.
  - Checks: same seam priorities as stripes, plus a stricter both-axis match at matched seams.

================================================================================
GOTCHAS THAT COST TIME THIS SESSION
================================================================================
- audit/panel_angles.py (NOT pattern_map.py) is the tool for per-panel angles. It's fixed; the
  old coherence math read background + silhouette edges as false "pattern".
- Automated structure-tensor angle measurement is reliable ONLY on (a) clean studio product
  photography on a plain bg and (b) isolated CDN layers with a real alpha channel. It is NOT
  reliable on casual/editorial photos — texture/lighting/JPEG noise defeat it, and it silently
  locks onto a SILHOUETTE EDGE instead of the pattern. For those, settle VISUALLY.
- Single-dominant-angle tools do NOT work on 2D check patterns AT ALL (a check has two line sets).
  Measure checks by eye, or with a proper two-axis method (the blur-based coarse/fine split fails
  because sparse grid lines die under blur faster than dense weave texture).
- SS live browsing: DOM image reads need an explicit wait + re-read (race conditions gave a false
  "8-layer" count once — real count is 11). Some fabric SKUs fall back to a generic placeholder
  image in the custom-made tool (not real per-garment assets) — verify the layer URL isn't
  /custommade/assets/default-images/.
- Playbook measurement rules still hold: compare at matched px/cm; hard-mask alpha>200 + erode
  3px; always LOOK (6× zoom), don't trust a metric alone; period.py is unreliable.
